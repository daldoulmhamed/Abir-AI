## Lesson 2.1: Advanced Prompting for Content Creation

### The Content Creation Framework

To generate high-quality content consistently, use the **CRAFT framework**:

**C - Context**: What's the background?  
**R - Role**: What perspective should the AI take?  
**A - Audience**: Who is this for?  
**F - Format**: How should it be structured?  
**T - Tone**: What's the voice and style?  

### Advanced Prompting Techniques

#### Technique 1: Multi-Step Instructions

Break complex tasks into clear steps within a single prompt.

**Example:**
```
Write a LinkedIn post about AI productivity tools. Follow these steps:

1. Start with an attention-grabbing question or statistic
2. Share 3 specific tools with brief descriptions (max 30 words each)
3. Include a personal insight or lesson learned
4. End with a call-to-action asking readers to share their favorite tools
5. Use 2-3 relevant hashtags

Length: 150-200 words
Tone: Professional but conversational
Audience: Mid-level professionals in tech and business
```

#### Technique 2: Voice Cloning with Examples

Teach the AI your writing style by providing examples.

**Example:**
```
I want you to write in my style. Here are 3 examples of my writing:

[Example 1: Your previous email/post]
[Example 2: Another sample]
[Example 3: Third sample]

Now write a similar piece about [topic], maintaining the same tone, sentence structure, and vocabulary level.
```

#### Technique 3: Iterative Refinement

Guide the AI through multiple versions.

**Example:**
```
Write a product description for [product].

Requirements:
- 100 words maximum
- Focus on benefits, not features
- Use power words (e.g., "transform", "effortless", "proven")
- Include social proof or a statistic
- End with urgency (limited time, exclusive offer)

After you write it, I'll ask you to:
1. Make it more concise
2. Adjust the tone
3. Add specific details
```

#### Technique 4: Constraint-Based Prompting

Use creative constraints to generate unique content.

**Example:**
```
Write an email introducing our new software update. Constraints:

Must include:
- A metaphor or analogy in the first paragraph
- Exactly 3 new features, each explained in one sentence
- A benefit statement for each feature
- No buzzwords or jargon

Must NOT include:
- More than 150 words
- Exclamation marks
- Generic phrases like "we're excited to announce"

Tone: Friendly and confident
```

#### Technique 5: Role-Play Scenarios

Have AI adopt specific expert personas.

**Example:**
```
You are a senior copywriter at a top advertising agency with 15 years of experience in tech marketing. Your specialty is writing compelling ad copy that converts.

Write 5 different Google ad headlines (max 30 characters each) for a project management tool targeting startup founders.

For each headline, explain:
- Why it would resonate with the target audience
- What psychological principle it uses (urgency, social proof, pain point, etc.)
```

## Lesson 2.2: Maintaining Your Voice & Brand Consistency

### Why Voice Matters

When using AI for content creation, the biggest challenge is maintaining authenticity. Generic AI content is recognizable and unengaging. Your unique voice is your competitive advantage.

### Building a Voice Profile

Create a comprehensive voice profile to guide AI outputs.

**Voice Profile Template:**

```markdown
## My/Our Voice Profile

**Tone Characteristics:**
- [Adjective 1, e.g., Professional yet approachable]
- [Adjective 2, e.g., Confident but not arrogant]
- [Adjective 3, e.g., Informative with personality]

**Vocabulary Guidelines:**
- Words we USE often: [e.g., "proven", "practical", "actionable"]
- Words we AVOID: [e.g., "synergy", "revolutionary", "game-changer"]

**Sentence Structure:**
- Average sentence length: [e.g., 12-18 words]
- Paragraph length: [e.g., 2-4 sentences]
- Question usage: [e.g., rhetorical questions to engage]

**Content Patterns:**
- Opening style: [e.g., Start with a relatable scenario or surprising fact]
- Structure: [e.g., Use subheadings every 2-3 paragraphs]
- Closing style: [e.g., End with actionable takeaway]

**Perspective:**
- First person (I/we) vs. Third person: [Your preference]
- Active vs. Passive voice: [Prefer active voice]

**Humor & Emotion:**
- Use of humor: [e.g., Subtle humor, no sarcasm]
- Emotional appeals: [e.g., Empathy and understanding over fear]

**Examples:**
[Paste 2-3 samples of your best writing]
```

### Voice Calibration Prompt

Use this template to train AI on your voice:

```
I need you to write in my specific style. Here's my voice profile:

[Paste your voice profile from above]

Before writing any content for me:
1. Confirm you understand my voice characteristics
2. Ask clarifying questions if anything is unclear
3. Provide a brief sample (50 words) for me to approve

Once I approve, you can proceed with the full content.
```

### Brand Consistency Techniques

#### Technique 1: Brand Guideline Integration

**Prompt Example:**
```

TONE: Professional, empowering, results-oriented

- Use "you" language (speak directly to reader)
- One idea per paragraph
- Include specific examples and numbers
- No hyperbole or exaggeration

Write [content type] following these exact guidelines.
```

**Content Checklist:**

## Lesson 2.3: Content Types & Templates

### Professional Emails

#### Email Type 1: Standard Business Email

**Template Prompt:**
```
Write a professional email with the following elements:

PURPOSE: [State the reason for the email]
RECIPIENT: [Describe the person/role]
KEY POINTS: [List 2-4 main points to cover]
DESIRED ACTION: [What you want them to do]
TONE: [Professional/Formal/Friendly]
LENGTH: [Word count limit]

Include:
- Clear subject line
- Appropriate greeting
- Concise body with paragraphs
- Professional closing
```

**Example Use:**
```
Write a professional email with the following elements:

PURPOSE: Request a meeting to discuss project timeline concerns
RECIPIENT: Project manager, collaborative relationship
KEY POINTS:
- Original deadline may not be realistic
- Three specific bottlenecks causing delays
- Proposed alternative timeline
DESIRED ACTION: Schedule 30-minute meeting this week
TONE: Professional but friendly, solution-focused
LENGTH: 150-200 words
```

#### Email Type 2: Difficult Conversations

**Template Prompt:**
```
Write a diplomatic email addressing a sensitive issue:

SITUATION: [Describe the problem/concern]
RELATIONSHIP: [Your relationship with recipient]
YOUR GOAL: [Desired outcome]
CONSTRAINTS:
- Must acknowledge their perspective
- Should use "I" statements, not accusations
- Offer specific solutions
- Maintain positive relationship

Tone: Empathetic, professional, constructive
Length: 200-250 words
```

#### Email Type 3: Outreach/Networking

**Template Prompt:**
```
Write a networking/outreach email:

RECIPIENT: [Description and why you're contacting them]
CONNECTION POINT: [Mutual contact, shared interest, or relevant context]
VALUE PROPOSITION: [What's in it for them?]
SPECIFIC ASK: [Clear, reasonable request]

Requirements:
- Personalized opening (no generic "I hope this email finds you well")
- Brief introduction (who you are in one sentence)
- Clear value exchange
- Easy way to respond (yes/no question or specific time options)
- Maximum 120 words
```

### Reports & Documents

#### Report Template: Executive Summary

**Prompt:**
```
Create an executive summary for [topic/project]:

KEY SECTIONS:
1. Overview (2-3 sentences: what, why, when)
2. Key Findings (3-5 bullet points)
3. Recommendations (numbered list, 3-4 items)
4. Next Steps (what happens now)

DATA TO INCLUDE:
[Provide your specific data, metrics, dates]

FORMAT:
- Use headers for each section
- Bold important numbers/dates
- One paragraph maximum per section (except findings/recommendations)
- Total length: 300-400 words

AUDIENCE: [Senior management/Team/Stakeholders]
TONE: [Professional, data-driven, confident]
```

#### Report Template: Project Update

**Prompt:**
```
Write a project status update report:

PROJECT: [Name and brief description]
REPORTING PERIOD: [Date range]

INCLUDE:
1. Progress Summary
   - Completed milestones: [List them]
   - In progress: [List them]
   - Not started: [List them]

2. Metrics
   - [Metric 1]: [Value] (vs. target: [target])
   - [Metric 2]: [Value] (vs. target: [target])

3. Challenges & Risks
   - [Challenge 1]: Impact and mitigation plan
   - [Challenge 2]: Impact and mitigation plan

4. Next Period Priorities
   - [3-5 key priorities]

FORMAT: Use tables for metrics, bullet points for lists
LENGTH: 500-600 words
TONE: Objective, transparent, action-oriented
```

### Social Media Content

#### LinkedIn Post Template

**Prompt:**
```
Create a LinkedIn post about [topic]:

STRUCTURE:
- Hook (first line must grab attention‚Äîuse a question, statistic, or bold statement)
- Personal story or insight (2-3 sentences)
- Value/lesson (3-4 key points with line breaks for readability)
- Call-to-action (ask a question or encourage sharing)

REQUIREMENTS:
- 150-200 words
- Use short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each)
- Include 1-2 emojis (subtle, professional)
- Add 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end
- Write for [target audience]

TONE: [Professional/Thought leadership/Conversational]
PERSPECTIVE: First-person experience sharing
```

#### Twitter/X Thread Template

**Prompt:**
```
Create a Twitter/X thread about [topic] with [5-7] tweets:

THREAD STRUCTURE:
Tweet 1: Hook + promise (what they'll learn)
Tweets 2-[N-1]: Main points (one key idea per tweet)
Final Tweet: Summary + CTA

REQUIREMENTS:
- Each tweet: 200-280 characters (leave room for replies)
- Use numbers/lists where appropriate
- One tweet should include a relevant statistic or data point
- Make it thread-worthy (build curiosity, progressive value)
- Conversational tone, easy to read

TOPIC: [Your specific topic]
AUDIENCE: [Who this is for]
GOAL: [Educate/Inspire/Start conversation]
```

### Marketing & Sales Copy

#### Product Description Template

**Prompt:**
```
Write a product description for [product name]:

PRODUCT DETAILS:
- Category: [Type of product]
- Key features: [List features]
- Main benefits: [List benefits]
- Target customer: [Description]
- Price point: [Range]

STRUCTURE:
1. Attention-grabbing headline (6-10 words)
2. Problem statement (What pain does it solve?) - 1-2 sentences
3. Solution/benefit statements (How it helps) - 3-4 bullet points
4. Social proof (Include: [testimonial/statistic/achievement])
5. Call-to-action (Clear next step)

LENGTH: 150-200 words
TONE: [Persuasive but not pushy/Benefits-focused/Customer-centric]
AVOID: Technical jargon, vague claims, overused phrases
```

#### Landing Page Copy Template

**Prompt:**
```
Write landing page copy for [offer/product]:

PAGE SECTIONS:

1. HERO SECTION:
   - Headline: Clear value proposition (8-12 words)
   - Subheadline: Expand on the benefit (15-20 words)
   - CTA button text: Action-oriented (2-4 words)

2. PROBLEM SECTION:
   - Describe the pain point (2-3 sentences)
   - Agitate slightly (why current solutions fail)

3. SOLUTION SECTION:
   - How [product] solves this (3 key benefits)
   - Each benefit: heading + 2 sentences

4. HOW IT WORKS:
   - 3-4 simple steps
   - Each step: number + heading + brief description

5. SOCIAL PROOF:
   - 2-3 testimonial snippets (quote format)
   - Include name and context

6. FINAL CTA:
   - Urgency statement
   - Clear action button
   - Risk reversal (money-back guarantee, free trial, etc.)

TARGET AUDIENCE: [Description]
TONE: Confident, benefit-focused, conversational
TOTAL LENGTH: 600-800 words
```

## Tips & Common Mistakes

### üí° Pro Tips

1. **Save Your Best Prompts**: Build a library of tested templates
2. **Combine AI + Human Editing**: Always add your personal touch
3. **Use Conversation Memory**: Build on previous responses for better context
4. **Test Multiple Variations**: Generate 3-5 versions, pick the best, combine elements
5. **Version Control**: Keep track of which prompts produce the best results
6. **Specificity Wins**: The more details you provide, the better the output
7. **Edit, Don't Accept Blindly**: Always review and personalize AI content
